Flexible PCB for esp32 chip:

1. Pin 32 controls a n-mosfet that can turn on a load on the 3.7v lipo (or 5v if
a converter is soldered on) and there is a flywheel diode, so preferable for
inductive loads.
2. Pins 33, 25 and 26 each control a n-channel mosfet on the 3.7 or 5v load.
Grouped together in one 4-pin header and easy to use for RGB LED.
3. Pin 16 controls an n-mosfet that enables power to 3 headers, each with 4
pins (useful for powering on the sensors just to read them and then cutting the power
to conserve battery):
 - pin 34, 35, 3.3v and gnd
 - pin 17, 18, 3.3v and gnd
 - pin 36, 39, 3.3v and gnd
4. One header with TX, RX, 3.3v and gnd - used for reprogramming
5. Pins 13 and 14 are left as just open holes and can be soldered to easily.
